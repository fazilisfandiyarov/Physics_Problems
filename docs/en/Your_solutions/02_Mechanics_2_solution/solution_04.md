# Physics Analysis: Energy and Momentum

This report calculates the final velocity of two blocks following a slide and an inelastic collision.

## 1. Phase One: The Slide (Conservation of Energy)
The first block ($m_1 = 0.5 \text{ kg}$) starts at height $h = 3.0 \text{ m}$. Ignoring friction, we find its velocity at the bottom ($v_1$):

$$v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2 \times 9.81 \times 3.0} \approx 7.67 \text{ m/s}$$

## 2. Phase Two: The Collision (Conservation of Momentum)
The moving block hits a stationary block ($m_2 = 1.5 \text{ kg}$) and they stick together.

**Variables:**
* $m_1 = 0.5 \text{ kg}$
* $m_2 = 1.5 \text{ kg}$
* $v_1 = 7.67 \text{ m/s}$
* $v_2 = 0 \text{ m/s}$

**Inelastic Collision Formula:**
$$m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)V_{final}$$

**Calculation:**
$$(0.5 \times 7.67) + 0 = (0.5 + 1.5)V_{final}$$
$$3.835 = 2.0 \times V_{final}$$
$$V_{final} \approx 1.92 \text{ m/s}$$
