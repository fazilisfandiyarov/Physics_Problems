## 5. Relative Velocity: River Crossing

We are given a river flowing east and a boat that needs to travel directly North (perpendicular to the current).

**Given constants:**
* Velocity of the river ($\vec{v}_r$): $2 \text{ m/s}$ [East]
* Speed of the boat in still water ($v_b$): $5 \text{ m/s}$
* Width of the river ($w$): $200 \text{ m}$

### 1. Determining the Direction (Angle)
To move directly North, the boat must head at an angle $\theta$ **West of North** to counteract the river's eastward flow. The horizontal component of the boat's velocity must exactly equal the river's speed.

Let $\theta$ be the angle measured from the North towards the West:
$$\sin\theta = \frac{v_{river}}{v_{boat}}$$
$$\sin\theta = \frac{2}{5} = 0.4$$

Calculating the angle:
$$\theta = \arcsin(0.4) \approx 23.58^\circ$$

**Conclusion:** The boat should head approximately **$23.58^\circ$ West of North**.

---

### 2. Resultant Velocity (Northward)
The boat's effective speed moving directly North ($v_{resultant}$) is the vertical component of its velocity. We can use the Pythagorean theorem:
$$v_{resultant} = \sqrt{v_{boat}^2 - v_{river}^2}$$
$$v_{resultant} = \sqrt{5^2 - 2^2} = \sqrt{25 - 4} = \sqrt{21}$$
$$v_{resultant} \approx 4.58 \text{ m/s}$$

---

### 3. Time to Cross
The time ($t$) taken to cross the river depends only on the northward component of the velocity and the width of the river:
$$t = \frac{\text{Width}}{v_{resultant}}$$
$$t = \frac{200}{\sqrt{21}} \approx \frac{200}{4.58}$$
$$t \approx 43.64 \text{ seconds}$$
