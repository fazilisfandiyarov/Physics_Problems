## 8. Circular Motion: Centripetal Acceleration at the Equator

To calculate the centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) of a person standing on the Earth's equator, we use the kinematics of uniform circular motion.

### 1. Given Data
* **Earth's Radius ($R$):** $6378 \text{ km} = 6,378,000 \text{ m}$
* **Rotation Period ($T$):** The Earth completes one rotation in approximately 24 hours.
  $$T = 24 \times 3600 = 86,400 \text{ s}$$

---

### 2. Formula Derivation
The centripetal acceleration can be expressed in terms of tangential velocity ($v$) or angular velocity ($\omega$):
$$a_c = \frac{v^2}{R} = \omega^2 R$$

First, we find the angular velocity $\omega$:
$$\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T}$$

Substituting this into the acceleration formula:
$$a_c = \left( \frac{2\pi}{T} \right)^2 R = \frac{4\pi^2 R}{T^2}$$

---

$$a_c = \frac{4 \times \pi^2 \times 6,378,000}{(86,400)^2}$$
