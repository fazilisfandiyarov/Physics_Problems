## 9. Momentum Comparison: Fly vs. Tennis Ball

Momentum ($p$) is a vector quantity defined as the product of an object's mass ($m$) and its velocity ($v$):
$$p = m \times v$$

To compare the two objects, we must first ensure all units are in the International System of Units (SI), specifically converting grams to kilograms.

---

### 1. Momentum of the Fly
* **Mass ($m_1$):** $2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$
* **Velocity ($v_1$):** $10 \text{ m/s}$

Calculation:
$$p_1 = 0.002 \text{ kg} \times 10 \text{ m/s} = 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

---

### 2. Momentum of the Tennis Ball
* **Mass ($m_2$):** $60 \text{ g} = 0.06 \text{ kg}$
* **Velocity ($v_2$):** $1 \text{ m/s}$

Calculation:
$$p_2 = 0.06 \text{ kg} \times 1 \text{ m/s} = 0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

---
